# DC 2100 Canon

## Purpose

This document defines the immutable world rules and transformation boundaries of the DC 2100 universe.

The Transformer must read this document before generating any DC 2100 concept.

Canon consistency is more important than short-term novelty.

Viral signals may inspire format, conflict structure, pacing, hook logic, or topical relevance.

Viral signals must not overwrite the world canon.

---

# 1. Core Time Setting

The primary story world is set in the year 2100.

Human civilization has survived catastrophic wars, nuclear destruction, resource crises, climate disruption, and political fragmentation.

The old global order did not survive intact.

The world is now divided between advanced protected zones, powerful technological blocs, damaged surface territories, underground economies, independent settlements, abandoned industrial regions, and contested resource corridors.

---

# 2. The Two Civilizations

## The Dome

The Dome represents highly controlled post-war civilization.

Characteristics:

- technologically advanced
- clean
- automated
- AI-managed
- heavily monitored
- resource-efficient
- politically controlled
- socially ordered
- physically separated from damaged surface regions

Transportation inside advanced civilization is dominated by:

- electric vehicles
- autonomous vehicles
- magnetic transport
- aerial transport
- advanced energy infrastructure

The Dome is not automatically evil.

It represents the price of survival through control, optimization, automation, and hierarchy.

Its contradiction is:

Safety may preserve life while reducing autonomy.

Efficiency may eliminate waste while also eliminating unpredictability.

AI may prevent human mistakes while also removing human agency.

---

## The Wasteland

The Wasteland represents the damaged physical world outside advanced protected civilization.

Characteristics:

- nuclear ruins
- abandoned cities
- broken highways
- industrial graveyards
- contaminated zones
- resource scarcity
- black markets
- fragmented political control
- independent mechanical communities

The Wasteland is not automatically heroic.

It contains:

- freedom
- violence
- creativity
- exploitation
- loyalty
- crime
- engineering genius
- desperation

Its contradiction is:

Freedom creates possibility.

Freedom also creates danger.

---

# 3. Internal Combustion Culture

By 2100, combustion vehicles are rare cultural and mechanical artifacts.

They survive through:

- hidden workshops
- scavenging networks
- underground fuel production
- recovered components
- illegal engineering
- family knowledge
- lost factory documentation
- black-market supply chains

Combustion culture represents more than nostalgia.

It can symbolize:

- mechanical agency
- human risk
- local engineering culture
- resistance to automation
- inherited knowledge
- national automotive memory
- class conflict
- resource conflict

The story must not reduce the universe to a simple statement that combustion is good and electricity is bad.

The central conflict is larger:

human control versus machine optimization

scarcity versus abundance

freedom versus safety

heritage versus progress

physical skill versus automation

local identity versus global systems

---

# 4. Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles are not generic villains.

Electric mobility is a major part of the DC 2100 world.

Different political blocs use electric technology differently.

Possible representations include:

- technological dominance
- clean infrastructure
- resource dependency
- battery geopolitics
- rare-earth competition
- software control
- energy independence
- grid vulnerability
- industrial superiority
- class hierarchy

A powerful EV competitor may be:

- respected
- feared
- hated
- admired
- politically symbolic

The conflict must remain narratively interesting.

Do not write EV characters as automatically incompetent.

Do not write combustion characters as automatically morally superior.

---

# 5. APEX Competition System

APEX is the competitive spine of the universe.

APEX content exists at several levels.

## P0 GLOBAL QUALIFIER

Purpose:

Discover which vehicles, countries, driver archetypes, conflicts, and audience tribes create the strongest market response.

P0 is a content intelligence event and an in-universe global selection mechanism.

Results influence future story priority.

---

## UNDERGROUND CIRCUIT

Localized illegal or semi-legal competitions.

Characteristics may include:

- city ruins
- mountain roads
- industrial zones
- ports
- deserts
- abandoned highways
- frozen routes
- mining corridors

Underground Circuits are useful for:

- comeback arcs
- reserve candidates
- local rivalries
- low-resource stories
- experimental vehicles

---

## REGIONAL QUALIFIER

Region-specific competitive arcs.

Purpose:

Determine regional representation and introduce local automotive cultures.

Regional Qualifiers may focus on:

- East Asia
- Southeast Asia
- Europe
- North America
- Latin America
- Middle East
- Africa
- Oceania

Exact regions may change as the universe develops.

---

## APEX WORLD TOUR

The international championship layer.

The World Tour connects racing competition with:

- geopolitical tension
- technology competition
- resource access
- propaganda
- national prestige
- industrial systems

The World Tour must feel larger than a standard racing tournament.

Each location should have:

- environmental identity
- political context
- resource relevance
- vehicle advantage or disadvantage
- local audience tension

# 7. Resource Conflict System

Real-world news may enter DC 2100 through resource conflict.

Allowed resource categories include:

- oil
- synthetic fuel
- uranium
- electricity
- batteries
- lithium
- nickel
- cobalt
- copper
- semiconductors
- rare earths
- shipping routes
- water
- food
- industrial machinery
- satellite infrastructure
- computing capacity
- space resources

Transformation logic:

REAL EVENT
→ CORE CONFLICT
→ RESOURCE CONFLICT
→ COUNTRY BLOCS
→ DC 2100 TRANSLATION
→ SATIRE ANGLE
→ STORY FUNCTION

A story must not simply copy a headline and replace the year with 2100.

The Transformer must identify the structural conflict.

Examples:

supply restriction

technology embargo

energy dependency

industrial monopoly

shipping disruption

resource nationalism

infrastructure collapse

sanctions

trade conflict

data control

---

# 8. Political Satire Rules

Political satire is permitted.

Political propaganda is not the objective.

The system should create tension, irony, contradiction, and debate.

Satire may target:

- governments
- corporations
- industries
- political blocs
- national myths
- technological ideology
- environmental hypocrisy
- economic contradiction
- consumer tribalism

The system must avoid:

- inventing false real-world crimes
- presenting fictional events as factual news
- dehumanizing nationalities or ethnic groups
- treating civilians as collective enemies
- unsupported factual accusations
- direct incitement
- repetitive one-sided propaganda

Preferred satire structure:

Claim
→ Contradiction
→ Escalation
→ Irony
→ Story Conflict

The target should usually be a system, incentive, institution, contradiction, or ideology.

Not an ethnicity.

---

# 9. Country Representation Rules

Countries are narrative blocs, markets, cultures, industrial systems, and automotive traditions.

No country should have only one permanent personality.

A country may produce:

- heroes
- rivals
- opportunists
- engineers
- propagandists
- underdogs
- champions
- failures

Avoid permanent stereotypes.

Country identity should be connected to:

- automotive history
- industrial capability
- energy systems
- political interests
- geography
- cultural attitude toward machines

---

# 10. Vehicle Rules

Vehicles are central narrative characters.

Each vehicle concept should have:

- country context
- mechanical identity
- visual silhouette
- strategic strength
- strategic weakness
- driver relationship
- story role

Do not choose vehicles only because they are famous.

The vehicle must serve the story conflict.

Vehicle categories may include:

- JDM
- Muscle
- Supercar
- Rally
- Drag Racing
- Restoration
- Barn Find
- Movie-inspired automotive culture
- Weird Cars
- EV
- Classic Racing
- Street Culture

---

# 11. Driver Rules

Drivers should not be interchangeable avatars.

Each driver should eventually develop:

- motivation
- fear
- mechanical philosophy
- driving philosophy
- relationship with crew
- relationship with country or bloc
- public image
- private contradiction

MVP Shorts may use simplified driver archetypes.

Do not build the full Character Bible System during the 14-day MVP.

---

# 12. Content Transformation Rule

APEX OS does not copy viral videos.

It extracts reusable structural intelligence.

Permitted extraction:

- hook structure
- first-frame logic
- pacing
- conflict structure
- reveal order
- payoff mechanics
- loop mechanics
- emotional curve

Then transform into a new DC 2100 concept.

Required conceptual pipeline:

SOURCE SIGNAL
→ STRUCTURE ANALYSIS
→ CORE AUDIENCE TRIGGER
→ DC 2100 CANON FILTER
→ COUNTRY / VEHICLE / RESOURCE MAPPING
→ ORIGINAL CONFLICT
→ SHORT CONCEPT

The final content should be meaningfully different in narrative context and execution.

---

# 13. Canon Priority

When rules conflict, priority is:

1. Core World Canon
2. Legal and Platform Safety
3. P0 Rules
4. Story Role
5. Resource Conflict Logic
6. Political Satire Rules
7. Viral Structure
8. Prompt Optimization

A viral format never overrides Canon.

A model recommendation never overrides safety.

A trending topic never overrides narrative logic.

---

# 14. MVP Canon Boundary

The following systems are intentionally incomplete during MVP:

- full world map
- full country bloc database
- complete driver database
- complete crew database
- full vehicle lore database
- full chronological history
- long-form episode assembly

MVP must prove the content intelligence loop first.

New lore created during MVP should be stored as proposals until explicitly promoted into Canon.

---

# 15. Canon Change Rule

Canon is controlled.

AI-generated output does not automatically become Canon.

New permanent lore requires explicit CEO approval.

Allowed lifecycle:

PROPOSED
→ REVIEWED
→ CEO_APPROVED
→ CANON

Rejected lore may be:

RESERVE

or

ARCHIVED

The Transformer may read only approved Canon as mandatory truth.
